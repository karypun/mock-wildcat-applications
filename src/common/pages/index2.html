This is a mock internal service to review applications and decide whether a licence should be granted for owning a wildcat.

Some wildcats are classified as very dangerous and will require a Dangerous Wild Animal licence to keep them.

In the UK, some domesticated breeds such as Savannah cats will require a licence depending on what generation they are. Make sure you know the filial number of your cat from the breeder you are buying from.

Help with filial numbers
An F1 generation cat is the first cross between the wild African serval and the domestic cat and are 50% wild. These will require a licence. However, cats further down the generations, from F2 to F4 can be legally owned without a licence.
